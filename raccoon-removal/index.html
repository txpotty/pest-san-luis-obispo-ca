Understanding the Signs of a Raccoon Infestation and What They Mean

Recognizing the early signs of a raccoon infestation is important for addressing the problem quickly and minimizing potential damage. Raccoons are nocturnal creatures, so you may hear or see them more often during the evening hours. Being aware of their common behaviors and signs can help you determine if you need professional raccoon removal in San Luis Obispo, CA.

Nocturnal Noises in Attic or Walls

One of the most common indicators of raccoons on your property is hearing noises in your attic, walls, or chimney at night. These sounds can include scratching, thumping, chattering, or even growling. Raccoons are quite active at night, especially when they are nesting or searching for food. Hearing these sounds consistently is a strong sign that you have unwanted visitors. This is different from the sounds of rodents like mice or rats, which tend to be more scurrying and gnawing. Raccoon noises are often heavier and more deliberate.

Damaged Garbage Cans or Scattered Trash

Raccoons are well-known for raiding garbage cans. If you find your trash cans overturned, lids removed, or garbage scattered around your property frequently, raccoons are likely the culprits. They are attracted to the food scraps in your trash and have strong paws and clever minds, making them adept at accessing poorly secured bins. This can create an unsightly mess and attract other wildlife as well.

Presence of Droppings

Raccoon droppings, also known as scat, can be found in or around your property. They often establish communal latrines, areas where they repeatedly deposit their waste. These latrines can be found on decks, patios, in attics, near trees, or along fences. Raccoon droppings can be cylindrical and vary in size depending on their diet. It's important to handle raccoon droppings with caution as they can contain harmful parasites like roundworms, which can pose serious health risks to humans and pets. If you suspect you've found a raccoon latrine, avoid direct contact and contact a professional for safe removal and sanitation.

Structural Damage (Attic, Roof, Vents)

Raccoons are strong and persistent, and they can cause significant structural damage to gain entry into your home or to create nesting sites. This can include tearing apart shingles, fascia boards, soffits, vent screens, or even chewing through wiring. Damage to these areas not only provides entry points for raccoons but can also lead to further issues like water leaks and energy inefficiency. Inspecting your roofline, attic, and crawl spaces regularly can help you spot this damage early.

Unusual Odors

A strong, unpleasant odor on your property could indicate a raccoon presence. This odor can come from their urine, feces, or even a deceased animal within a wall or attic space. Raccoon urine has a distinct musky smell that can permeate insulation and building materials. These odors can be persistent and difficult to eliminate without professional help and proper sanitation. The smell can also attract other pests.

Tracks or Paw Prints

Looking for tracks can help confirm the presence of raccoons. Raccoon paw prints are distinctive, resembling small human hands with five long toes. They are often found in mud, dust, or snow around your property, particularly near potential food sources or entry points. Observing tracks can give you an idea of the size and activity level of the raccoons.

Witnessing Raccoons on Your Property

While raccoons are nocturnal, you might occasionally see them during the day, especially if they are foraging for food or have been disturbed. Seeing a raccoon in your yard, on your deck, or near your refuse bins, particularly multiple times, is a clear indication of an issue. While a single sighting might not be a cause for alarm, repeated sightings or seeing them acting aggressively should prompt you to seek professional help for raccoon removal.

Presence of Nests

Raccoons often build nests in attics, chimneys, sheds, or under decks. These nests can be made of leaves, twigs, insulation, or other debris. Discovering a nest on your property, especially in a contained space like an attic, is a clear sign of an infestation. Mother raccoons will build nests to raise their young, and the presence of a nest indicates a breeding population on your property.

Aggressive Behavior

While raccoons are generally not aggressive towards humans unless provoked, a mother raccoon protecting her young can be defensive. Disturbed raccoons may hiss, growl, or even charge. If you encounter a raccoon acting aggressively, it's crucial to maintain a safe distance and avoid attempting to handle the situation yourself. Professional wildlife control services are equipped to handle aggressive animals safely.

Damage to Gardens or Crops

Raccoons are opportunistic eaters and will readily feast on fruits, vegetables, and corn grown in gardens. If you notice your garden is being raided and damaged, and you see signs of raccoons, they are likely responsible. This can be frustrating for gardeners and can lead to significant loss of crops. Our services can help keep your garden protected from these persistent foragers. We can also assist with other common garden pests like mosquitoes if you're experiencing those issues.

Pet Food or Water Bowls Emptied

Leaving pet food or water bowls outside can attract raccoons. If you find your pet's food or water bowls are routinely emptied overnight, raccoons may be visiting your property for an easy meal or drink. Securing pet food indoors overnight can help deter them.

Unusual Pet Behavior

Your pets may also react to the presence of raccoons. Dogs may bark incessantly at the attic or walls, and cats may become agitated or try to get into areas where raccoons are present. Paying attention to your pet's unusual behavior can be an early warning sign of a wildlife issue. Your pets can also be affected by other pests like fleas or ticks, which we can also help with.

Damage to Fences or Decks

Raccoons can climb and are quite strong. They may damage fences or decks as they try to access areas or seek shelter. Look for claw marks, chewed wood, or broken boards on your outdoor structures. Repairing this damage is important to prevent further issues and maintain the integrity of your property.

Chimney Sounds

Raccoons sometimes use chimneys as nesting sites, especially mother raccoons looking for a secure place to raise their young. You might hear scratching, thumping, or even chattering sounds coming from your chimney. If you suspect a raccoon is in your chimney, do not light a fire, as this could harm the animal. Contact a professional for safe removal.

Odd Behavior of Raccoons

While less common, a raccoon behaving strangely during the daytime, appearing disoriented, or showing signs of aggression without provocation could indicate illness. It's essential to keep a safe distance from any wildlife exhibiting unusual behavior and report it to local animal control if you suspect the animal is sick. Never attempt to handle a potentially sick or injured raccoon yourself.
